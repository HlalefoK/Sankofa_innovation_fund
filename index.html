PEST ANALYSIS

Political

The war in Ukraine. Geo-political tension in Europe, Asia-Pasific(China-Taiwan tensions), coups in Sub-Saharan Africa with greater economic migration from Africa with greater economic migration from Africa to Europe, the USA migration pressure from South American neighbours are driving the greater political tensions and geo-political realignment.


South Africa and Africa are also caught up in risks of coups ans civil strife due to internal instability, high crime levels, poverty, low economic growth. Africa is projected to have the fastest growing population with over half being young people below the ages of 35.

Economic

Global economic slow-down, high inflation, high cost of living, with high unemployment and increased inequality in developing countires has increased pressures on countries. South Africa and Africa faces the greatest challenge of poverty, unemployment with food insecurity and crime reaching high levels.

The digitalisation or 4th Industrial revolution poses a great challenge whether it will lead to greater inequality by leaving the majority of citizens behind or it represent the possibility to play catch-up and leap-frog the development path through accelerated skills development and economic inclusion. The challenges of South Africa and Africa, with this high projected youth dividend must ramp up its promotion of innovative solutions with high impact wich will largely be driven by new technological developments.

Social

Inequality and exclusion of marginalised groups due to race, sexual-orientation, religion are some of the major global challenges today. South Africa has been reported as the most unequal society in the world, with unemployment hovering around 33% (expanded 44%-2023) with almost 50% youth unemployment.


Crime levels and gender-based violence are high, with children bearing the brunt of violent acts. Breakdown in basic service have led to sprawling informal sectors with unhealthy living conditions and high risk of breakout of pandemics. Early childhood development programs are ineffective, with primary school children lacking basic numeracy and literacy skills. Post-Covid pandemic recovery is slow, with its impact having been dire for employment and economic engagement of small businesses. There are serious 'wicked problems' needing urgent innovative solutions.

Technological

Technological advancement of the past decade or so have been profound, led by innovations in mobile devices and internet penetration. Africa has one of the highest internet and mobile handset penetration. Innovation in cloud storage as well as high capacity and high processing power of chips has been driving innovative customer-centric solutions,

with tech companies occupying the mojority of top position on the top 500 companies in the world. ML and AI has been on the rise due to technological developments in processing power and big data storage and analytics. The technological advancement provide an opportunity for innovative, efficient and cost-effective solutions to some of the 'wicked problems' facing society today. Leveraging these technological advancement and enabling access to 'understanding and consclous' capital, as well as appropriate skills program can lead to an accelerated pace of development of young companies addressing high impact solutions. Ensuring Diversity, Equity and inclusivity as a strategic objective will lead to broadening of economic participation and increase in local innovative solutions for sustained economic growth.

1. Application and review:

PROPOSED PROCESS

Funding applications for Qualifying Companies will be received through one of the 2 Deal Flow pipelines and will all need to contain the following checklist:
• Pitch Deck
• Company details
• Financial Statements
• Financial Projections

2. Presentation meeting:

PROPOSED PROCESS

Depending on whether the Pitch Deck received during the application process fits the criteria of Qualifying Companies that Sankofa is looking for at that point in time, a presentation meeting will be arranged between a minimum of 2 Sankofa Executive Team (and possibly external panel members who are experts in the Qualifying Company's field) and the management team of the Qualifying Company.

3. Assessment:

PROPOSED PROCESS

Assess
Advance - This will lead to phase 4 of the Opportunity Assessment process
Re-assess - This will result in as many follow up emails or meetings required to answer the necessary questions raised by the Executive Team
Recommend-IC - This will result in a decision to delay advancing the Qualifying Company with recommendations. The Qualifying Company’s application will be stored to undergo phase 2 at a later stage
Decline
Re-direct - This will result in a decision to decline the Qualifying Company and re-direct them to an entity within our network

4. Advance and Initial Offer:

PROPOSED PROCESS

Sankofa will present the Qualifying Company with a provisional letter and a term sheet (subject to terms such as due diligence outcomes and Board approval). These are non-binding documents but are advantageous as they not only create a feedback loop for the Qualifying Company but they also lay down the foundations of the terms of conditions for phase 7 of the process. The Qualifying Company can agree to advance to the due diligence phase, re-assess the terms on the Term Sheet or decline to continue.

5. Due Diligence:

PROPOSED PROCESS

Once a Qualifying Company gets to this phase of the assessment process, a comprehensive legal investigation will be conducted. The legal criteria will be assessed by external legal service providers. Depending on the outcome of this exercise, the Qualifying Company will be presented to the Investment Committee who will conclude one of 3 outcomes which are as follows:
Advance - This will result in the Investment Committee presenting the Qualifying Company for the Board’s consideration
Re-assess - This will result in as many follow up emails or meetings required to answer the necessary questions raised by the Investment Committee

6. Board of Director's consideration:

PROPOSED PROCESS

The Investment Committee will present the Qualifying Company to the Board for their consideration which will result in one of 2 outcomes:
Approval - This will result in the signing of binding agreements and the disbursement of funds thereafter
Decline - This will result in the in provisional letter and term sheet being withdrawn

7. Binding Agreements:

PROPOSED PROCESS

Once the binding agreements are signed, the Qualifying Company becomes the Qualifying Investment and will be a part of Sankofa’s Investment Portfolio. Sankofa will then disburse the funds to the Qualifying Investment as agreed, in exchange for equity and a board seat. The Post-InvestmentManagement process will commence from here
