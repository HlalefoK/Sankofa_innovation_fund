ABOUT SANKOFA INNOVATION FUND

Sankofa Innovation Fund, known as "Sankofa", is a dedicated transformation frontier fund that champions inclusive economic growth and shared prosperity by investing in innovative companies that offer high-impact solutions, particularly aimed at developing countries, with a specific focus on Africa. The fund prioritizes Diversity, Equity and Inclusivity (DEI) as a strategic cornerstone for sustainability and impactful outcomes, which forms a core component of its investment criteria. Sankofa is committed to a deliberate approach that ensures a well-rounded portfolio, inclusive of marginalized sectors and groups, with the dual objective of fostering social inclusivity and enhancing investor value. This targeted effort signifies the fund's dedication to promoting diversity, equity, and inclusivity within its investment strategy to drive positive economic development and societal impact.

SANKOFA'S EXTERNAL ENVIRONMENT:

PEST ANALYSIS

Political

The ongoing conflict in Ukraine, rising geopolitical unrest in various regions such as Europe and Asie-Pacific (including tensions between China and Taiwan), instances of coups in Sub-Saharan Africa, coupled with increased economic migration flows from Africa to Europe, and mounting migration pressures from South American nations towards the USA,


are fueling heightened political tensions and prompting shifts in globall geopolitical dynamics. South Africa and seveeral African nations are also grappling with the risks of coups and internal unrest, stemming from factors like structural instability, soaring crime rates, widespread poverty, and sluggish economic advancement. Africa, in particular, is forecasted to experience rapid population growth, with a significant proportion being youthful individuals under the age of 35.

Economic

The global economy is currrently experiencing a slowdown along with heightened inglation rates and a surge in the cost of living, exacerbating existing challenges such as high unemployment and increasing inequality, particularly in developing nations. South Africa and Africa are confronted with pronounced issues of poverty, unemployment, food insecurity,

and escalating crime levels. Amidst this backdrop, the digital transformation associated with the Fourth Industrial Revolution poses a significant dilemma. It raises the crucial question of whether this technological shift will widen existing disparities by marginalizing a significant portion of the population or if it can serve as an avenue to accelerate progress and enable leapfrogging in development through rapid skills enhancement and inclusive economic participation. Given the anticipated demographic dividend of youth in South Africa and Africa, there is a pressing need to intensify efforts towards fostering innovative solutions with substantial impacts, primarily fueled by emerging technological advancements.

Social

Social inequalities and the exclusion of marginalized groups based on factors such as race, sexual orientation, and religion represent significant global challenges in contemporary society. Notably, South Africa has been identified as the most unequal society worldwide, marked by an unemployment rate that currently sits at around 33%, projected to expand to 44% by 2023,


with nearly half of the youth population experiencing joblessness. Elevated levels of crime and gender-based violence persist, disproportionately affecting children who often endure the consequences of violent acts. The breakdown in essential services has led to the proliferation of informal settlements characterized by substandard living conditions, posing a high risk of disease outbreaks, including pandemics. Despite efforts, early childhood development programs have shown inefficacy, leaving primary school children deficient in fundamental numeracy and literacy skills. The post-Covid pandemic recovery process has been sluggish, severely impacting employment opportunities and economic prospects for small businesses. These challenges, often classified as 'wicked problems,' demand immediate attention and innovative solutions to address the pressing issues facing society.


Technological

Technological progress over the past decade has been remarkable, marked by significant advancements in mobile devices and the widespread adoption of the internet. Africa notably boasts high rates of internet connectivity and mobile device usage. Developments in cloud storage and the enhanced capabilities of chips, including high processing power and capacity,

have been instrumental in driving customer-focused innovations. Notably, tech companies are increasingly dominating the top positions among the world's 500 leading companies, fueled by continuous innovations. The increasing prominence of Machine Learning (ML) and Artificial Intelligence (AI) can be attributed to advancements in processing power, big data storage, and analytics. These technological strides present opportunities for the creation of innovative, efficient, and cost-effective solutions to tackle complex societal challenges. Harnessing these technological advancements and facilitating access to essential knowledge and skills, alongside fostering a culture of inclusivity and diversity, can accelerate the growth of emerging companies focused on delivering high-impact solutions. By prioritizing Diversity, Equity, and Inclusivity (DEI) as strategic imperatives, economies can achieve broader participation, drive local innovation, and sustain long-term economic growth.

1. Application and review:

PROPOSED PROCESS

Funding applications for Qualifying Companies will be received through one of the 2 Deal Flow pipelines and will all need to contain the following checklist:
• Pitch Deck
• Company details
• Financial Statements
• Financial Projections

2. Presentation meeting:

PROPOSED PROCESS

Depending on whether the Pitch Deck received during the application process fits the criteria of Qualifying Companies that Sankofa is looking for at that point in time, a presentation meeting will be arranged between a minimum of 2 Sankofa Executive Team (and possibly external panel members who are experts in the Qualifying Company's field) and the management team of the Qualifying Company.

3. Assessment:

PROPOSED PROCESS

Assess
Advance - This will lead to phase 4 of the Opportunity Assessment process
Re-assess - This will result in as many follow up emails or meetings required to answer the necessary questions raised by the Executive Team
Recommend-IC - This will result in a decision to delay advancing the Qualifying Company with recommendations. The Qualifying Company’s application will be stored to undergo phase 2 at a later stage
Decline
Re-direct - This will result in a decision to decline the Qualifying Company and re-direct them to an entity within our network

4. Advance and Initial Offer:

PROPOSED PROCESS

Sankofa will present the Qualifying Company with a provisional letter and a term sheet (subject to terms such as due diligence outcomes and Board approval). These are non-binding documents but are advantageous as they not only create a feedback loop for the Qualifying Company but they also lay down the foundations of the terms of conditions for phase 7 of the process. The Qualifying Company can agree to advance to the due diligence phase, re-assess the terms on the Term Sheet or decline to continue.

5. Due Diligence:

PROPOSED PROCESS

Once a Qualifying Company gets to this phase of the assessment process, a comprehensive legal investigation will be conducted. The legal criteria will be assessed by external legal service providers. Depending on the outcome of this exercise, the Qualifying Company will be presented to the Investment Committee who will conclude one of 3 outcomes which are as follows:
Advance - This will result in the Investment Committee presenting the Qualifying Company for the Board’s consideration
Re-assess - This will result in as many follow up emails or meetings required to answer the necessary questions raised by the Investment Committee

6. Board of Director's consideration:

PROPOSED PROCESS

The Investment Committee will present the Qualifying Company to the Board for their consideration which will result in one of 2 outcomes:
Approval - This will result in the signing of binding agreements and the disbursement of funds thereafter
Decline - This will result in the in provisional letter and term sheet being withdrawn

7. Binding Agreements:

PROPOSED PROCESS

Once the binding agreements are signed, the Qualifying Company becomes the Qualifying Investment and will be a part of Sankofa’s Investment Portfolio. Sankofa will then disburse the funds to the Qualifying Investment as agreed, in exchange for equity and a board seat. The Post-InvestmentManagement process will commence from here
